1. Electric Potential

Electric potential at a point in an electric field is the amount of work done in moving a unit positive charge from infinity to that point.
Hindi Explanation: Kisi electric field mein kisi point par "Electric Potential" ka matlab hai ki ek unit positive charge ko infinity se us point tak lane mein humein kitna kaam (work) karna pada. Ye batata hai ki us point par energy ka level kya hai.

Wq₀

2. Potential due to Point Charge

The electric potential at a distance 'r' from a single point charge 'q' is inversely proportional to the distance.
Hindi Explanation: Agar hamare paas ek akela charge 'q' hai, toh usse 'r' distance par potential nikalne ke liye hum integration ka use karte hain. Jaise-jaise distance (r) badhti hai, potential kam hota jata hai.

14πε₀
qr

3. Potential due to Multiple Charges

The total electric potential at a point due to a system of point charges is the algebraic sum of the potentials due to individual charges.
Hindi Explanation: Agar ek jagah bahut saare charges hain, toh kisi point par net potential nikalne ke liye hum har charge ka alag-alag potential nikalte hain aur unhe normal maths ki tarah (+ ya - sign ke saath) jod dete hain.
Vnet = V₁ + V₂ + V₃ + ...

4. Superposition Principle

It states that the electric potential at any point due to a group of charges is equal to the sum of the potentials of each charge, as if all other charges were absent.
Hindi Explanation: Ye principle kehta hai ki jab hum ek charge ka potential nikal rahe hote hain, toh doosre charges wahan ho ya na ho, usse pehle wale charge ke potential par koi asar nahi padta. Hum bas sabka individual potential nikal kar sum kar lete hain.

5. Potential Difference

The difference in electric potential between two points is the work done per unit charge to move it from one point to the other.
Hindi Explanation: Do points ke beech ka potential ka antar (difference). Ye hamein batata hai ki agar hum ek charge ko point A se point B tak le jayenge, toh humein kitna kaam karna padega.
ΔV = VB - VA =
WABq₀

6. Work Done in Moving Charge

Work done to move a charge 'q' through a potential difference ΔV is independent of the path taken.
Hindi Explanation: Kisi charge ko move karne mein jo work hota hai, wo sirf shuruati aur aakhiri point ke potential par depend karta hai. Aap chahe kisi bhi raste (path) se jao, work done same rahega.
W = q × ΔV

7. Electric Potential Energy

The total work done in assembling a system of charges by bringing them from infinity to their current positions.
Hindi Explanation: Jab hum charges ko door-door se lakar ek system banate hain (jaise ek triangle par set karna), toh us poore process mein jo total mehnat ya kaam hota hai, wahi us system ki Potential Energy ban jati hai.

8. Potential Energy of Two Charges

The energy of a system consisting of two point charges q₁ and q₂ separated by a distance 'r'.
Hindi Explanation: Do charges ke jode (pair) ki energy. Agar dono charge same sign ke hain toh energy positive hogi (ek doosre ko bhagayenge), aur agar opposite hain toh energy negative hogi (pyar/attraction).

14πε₀
q₁q₂r

The sum of the potential energies of all possible pairs of charges in the system.

1. Equipotential Surface

An equipotential surface is a surface on which the electric potential is the same at every point.
Hindi Explanation: Ye ek aisa surface (satah) hai jiske har ek point par electric potential ki value bilkul barabar hoti hai. Matlab agar aap is surface par ek jagah se doosri jagah jaoge, toh potential nahi badlega.
VA = VB = Constant

2. Properties of Equipotential Surface

Key properties include: No work is done in moving a charge, and electric field lines are always perpendicular to the surface.
Hindi Explanation: 1. Work Done Zero: Charge ko move karne mein koi kaam nahi karna padta ($W=0$).
2. Field Angle: Electric field lines hamesha is surface ke saath 90 degree ka angle banati hain.
3. Intersection: Do surfaces kabhi ek doosre ko nahi kaat-ti.

3. Equipotential Surface Shapes

The shape of the surface depends on the charge distribution, such as concentric spheres for a point charge.
Hindi Explanation: - Point Charge: Golaakaar (Spherical) shapes banti hain.
- Uniform Field: Seedhi plates (Parallel planes) banti hain.
- Line Charge: Cylinder ki tarah shapes banti hain.

4. Electric Field and Potential Relation

The electric field is related to the change in potential over a distance.
Hindi Explanation: Electric field ($E$) batata hai ki distance ke saath potential ($V$) kitni tezi se badal raha hai. Dono ke beech ka rishta bahut hi important formula se dikhaya jata hai.
E = -
dVdr

5. Negative Gradient Concept

The negative sign indicates that the direction of the electric field is in the direction of decreasing potential.
Hindi Explanation: Formula mein laga minus (-) sign ye dikhata hai ki Electric field hamesha us taraf jati hai jahan potential kam ho raha hota hai. Isse "Potential Gradient" bhi kehte hain.

6. Field from Potential Graph

The slope of a Potential vs. Distance graph gives the magnitude of the electric field.
Hindi Explanation: Agar hum $V$ aur $r$ ka graph banayein, toh us graph ki dhalan (slope) humein batati hai ki Electric field ki taqat (magnitude) kitni hai. Jitna teez dhalan, utni mazboot field.

7. Electric Dipole

An electric dipole consists of two equal and opposite charges separated by a small distance.
Hindi Explanation: Jab do barabar lekin ulte (+ aur -) charges ek doosre se thodi doori par rakhe hon, toh use dipole kehte hain. Iski taqat ko Dipole Moment ($p$) se napa jata hai.
p = q × 2a

8. Potential due to Dipole (Axial Position)

Electric potential at a point on the axis of the dipole.
Hindi Explanation: Dipole ki seedhi line (axis) par kisi point par potential nikalne ka formula. Yahan distance '$r$' dipole ke center se naapi jati hai.
V =
14πε₀
pr²

9. Potential due to Dipole (Equatorial)

Electric potential at any point on the equatorial line of a dipole is zero.
Hindi Explanation: Dipole ke bilkul beech se nikalne wali perpendicular line (equatorial line) par potential hamesha **Zero** hota hai. Kyunki wahan se dono charges ki doori barabar hoti hai aur potential ek doosre ko cancel kar dete hain.
Vequatorial = 0

1. P.E. of Dipole in External Field

The energy stored in a dipole when it is placed in an external electric field, calculated as the work done in rotating it.
Hindi: Jab dipole ko electric field mein rotate kiya jata hai, toh usme potential energy store hoti hai. Formula: $U = -pE \cos \theta$.
U = -pE cos θ

2. Conductors in Electrostatics

Materials which allow the flow of electric charges through them easily due to the presence of free electrons.
Hindi: Wo materials jinme electrons aazadi se move kar sakte hain. Static condition mein inke andar net electric field hamesha zero hoti hai.

3. Free Charges and Bound Charges

Free charges are mobile electrons in metals, whereas bound charges are fixed positive ions in the nucleus.
Hindi: Free Charges wo electrons hain jo poore conductor mein ghoom sakte hain. Bound Charges wo hote hain jo nucleus ke saath fix rehte hain.

4. Electrostatic Shielding

The method of protecting a certain region from external electric fields by enclosing it in a hollow conductor.
Hindi: Kisi sensitive device ko electric field se bachane ke liye use hollow conductor ke andar rakha jata hai, jise shielding kehte hain.

5. Properties of Conductors

Under electrostatic conditions, net charge inside a conductor is zero and the potential is constant throughout.
Hindi: Conductor ki khoobiyein: Andar net field zero hoti hai aur sara extra charge surface par distribute ho jata hai.

6. Electric Field Inside Conductor

In a static situation, the electric field intensity at any point inside the material of a conductor is zero.
Hindi: Static halat mein conductor ke andar koi net electric field nahi hoti, kyunki charges apne aap ko aise arrange kar lete hain ki field cancel ho jaye.

7. Charge Distribution on Surface

Any excess charge given to a conductor always resides entirely on its outer surface.
Hindi: Conductor ko diya gaya extra charge uske andar nahi rukta, balki repel hokar seedha bahar ki surface par aa jata hai.

8. Capacitance

Capacitance is the property of a conductor to store electric charge and electrical energy.
Hindi: Ye kisi conductor ki charge store karne ki kshamta (ability) hai. Iski unit Farad (F) hoti hai.
C = Q / V

9. Capacitance of Isolated Conductor

The capacitance of a single conductor, which depends on its size, shape, and surrounding medium.
Hindi: Ek akele (isolated) conductor ki capacitance uske geometry aur medium par nirbhar karti hai.

10. Capacitance of Spherical Conductor

The capacitance of a spherical conductor is directly proportional to its radius R.
Hindi: Ek R radius ke gole (sphere) ki capacitance ka formula: $C = 4 \pi \epsilon_0 R$.
C = 4πε₀ R

1. Capacitor

A capacitor is a device used to store electric charge and electrical energy in an electric field.
Hindi: Capacitor ek aisi device hai jo apne andar bijli (electric charge) aur energy ko jama karke rakhti hai. Isme do conductors hote hain jo ek doosre se thodi doori par hote hain.

2. Types of Capacitors

Capacitors are classified based on their shape and dielectric material, such as parallel plate, spherical, and cylindrical capacitors.
Hindi: Capacitors kai tarah ke hote hain: 1. Parallel Plate (do seedhi plates), 2. Spherical (golaakaar), aur 3. Cylindrical.

3. Parallel Plate Capacitor

A capacitor consisting of two large plane parallel conducting plates separated by a small distance.
Hindi: Isme do barabar ki metal plates ek doosre ke saamne rakhi jati hain. Ye sabse zyada use hone wala capacitor hai.

4. Capacitance without Dielectric

The capacitance of a parallel plate capacitor when the space between the plates is vacuum or air.
Hindi: Jab capacitor ki dono plates ke beech sirf hawa ya vacuum ho, tab uski capacitance ka formula ye hota hai:
C₀ =
ε₀ Ad

5. Capacitance with Dielectric

When a dielectric medium is placed between the plates, the capacitance increases by a factor of K.
Hindi: Agar plates ke beech koi material (dielectric) bhar diya jaye, toh capacitor ki taqat (capacitance) badh jati hai.
C = K C₀ =
K ε₀ Ad

6. Dielectric Material

Non-conducting materials (insulators) that can be polarized by an external electric field.
Hindi: Dielectric wo insulators hote hain jo current toh pass nahi karte, lekin electric field ka asar dikhate hain (jaise glass, plastic, mica).

7. Dielectric Constant (K)

The ratio of the capacitance with the dielectric medium to the capacitance with vacuum between the plates.
Hindi: Ye ek number hai jo batata hai ki kisi material ko capacitor mein dalne par uski capacitance kitne guna badh jayegi.
K =
CC₀

8. Polarization of Dielectric

The process of inducing dipoles in a dielectric material when subjected to an external electric field.
Hindi: Jab dielectric ko field mein rakhte hain, toh uske molecules set ho jate hain aur ek apni field banate hain. Isi process ko Polarization kehte hain.

1. Effect on Capacitance

When a dielectric is introduced between the plates of a capacitor, its capacitance increases by a factor of K.
Hindi Explanation: Jab plates ke beech dielectric slab rakhi jati hai, toh capacitor ki charge store karne ki kshamta (capacitance) badh jati hai.
C = K C₀

2. Effect on Electric Field

The internal electric field decreases due to the opposing field produced by the polarized dielectric.
Hindi Explanation: Dielectric ke polarization ki wajah se plates ke beech ki net electric field pehle se kam ho jati hai.
E = E₀ / K

3. Effect on Potential

The potential difference between the plates decreases when a dielectric is inserted (if the battery is disconnected).
Hindi Explanation: Kyunki field kam hoti hai, isliye plates ke beech ka voltage (Potential Difference) bhi kam ho jata hai.
V = V₀ / K

4. Capacitors in Series

In a series combination, the charge on each capacitor is the same, and the total potential is the sum of individual potentials.
Hindi Explanation: Jab capacitors ko ek ke baad ek ek hi line mein joda jata hai, toh sabpar charge (Q) same rehta hai lekin voltage bat jati hai.
1/Cₛ = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂ + ...

5. Capacitors in Parallel

In a parallel combination, the potential difference across each capacitor is the same, and the total charge is the sum of individual charges.
Hindi Explanation: Jab sabhi capacitors ke sire (ends) ek hi point se jude hon, toh unka voltage same rehta hai lekin total capacitance badh jati hai.
Cₚ = C₁ + C₂ + ...

6. Energy Stored in Capacitor

The work done in charging a capacitor is stored in the form of electrostatic potential energy between the plates.
Hindi Explanation: Capacitor ko charge karne mein jo mehnat (work) lagti hai, wahi uske andar energy ban kar ruk jati hai.
U = ½ CV² = ½ Q²/C

7. Energy Density

Energy stored per unit volume in the electric field between the plates of a capacitor.
Hindi Explanation: Capacitor ke andar ki jagah (volume) ke har ek tukde mein kitni energy hai, use hi Energy Density kehte hain.
u = ½ ε₀ E²

Q8. Explain Equipotential Surfaces and their key properties.
Definition A surface where electric potential is the same at every point. Properties (In Detail) 1. Work Done: Work done to move a charge on this surface is zero.
2. Direction: Electric field lines are always perpendicular to the surface.
3. No Crossing: Two equipotential surfaces can never intersect each other.
4. Field Strength: Surfaces are closer in strong field regions and farther in weak regions.
