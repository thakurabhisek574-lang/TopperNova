Electric Charges & Fields

TOPICS 1 - 4

Fundamental Nature of Charges

1. What is Electric Charge?

Electric charge is a fundamental physical property of matter that causes it to experience a force when placed in an electromagnetic field. Just as mass is responsible for gravitational interaction, electric charge is responsible for electrical interaction. In the International System of Units (SI), charge is measured in Coulombs (C).

2. Two Types of Charges

In nature, charges exist in two forms, which was famously categorized by Benjamin Franklin:

  • Positive Charge: Conventionally carried by protons. An atom becomes positive if it loses electrons.
  • Negative Charge: Conventionally carried by electrons. An atom becomes negative if it gains electrons.

3. Interaction Between Charges

The core law of electrostatics defines how charges behave near each other:

  • Law of Attraction: Unlike charges (Positive and Negative) attract each other.
  • Law of Repulsion: Like charges (Positive-Positive or Negative-Negative) repel each other.

4. Basic Properties of Electric Charge

Charge has unique characteristics: It is Scalar, it is Additivity (can be added algebraically), and it is Invariant (does not change with the speed of the object).

TOPICS 5 - 7

Core Laws of Electrostatics

5. Quantization of Charge

Quantization means that electric charge is not a continuous fluid. Instead, it exists in discrete packets. The smallest amount of charge that can exist independently is the charge of one electron ($e$). Any total charge on a body must be an integral multiple of this value.

Q = ± ne

Where n is an integer (1, 2, 3...) and $e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} C$.

6. Conservation of Charge

The total charge of an isolated system remains constant. Charge can be transferred from one object to another through various processes, but the net charge of the universe or an isolated system cannot be changed.

7. Additivity of Charges

Electric charge is additive in nature. If a system contains multiple charges, the total charge is found by calculating the algebraic sum (considering the + and - signs). Example: If charges are +2C, -5C, and +4C, the net charge is (+2 - 5 + 4) = +1C.

TOPICS 8 - 11

Charging Methods & Material Behavior

8. Methods of Charging

There are three primary ways to charge an uncharged body:

  • Charging by Friction: Transfer of electrons by rubbing two insulators together (e.g., Glass rod and Silk).
  • Charging by Conduction: Transfer of charge through direct physical contact between a charged conductor and a neutral one.
  • Charging by Induction: Bringing a charged object near a neutral conductor to redistribute its internal charges without touching.
  • Earthing: Connecting a charged body to the Earth to share or neutralize excess charges.

9. Conductors

Conductors are materials that allow electric charges to flow through them with minimal resistance. This is due to the presence of a high density of free electrons. Examples: Copper, Silver, Aluminum, and the Human Body.

10. Insulators (Dielectrics)

Insulators are materials that do not allow electricity to flow through them because their electrons are tightly bound to the nucleus. Examples: Rubber, Plastic, Wood, and Glass.

11. Free and Bound Charges

In conductors, the valence electrons are detached from the atoms and are Free Charges. In insulators, electrons are trapped within the atomic structure and are called Bound Charges.

TOPIC 1

1. Coulomb's Law Statement

Coulomb's Law electrostatics ka sabse mool (basic) kanoon hai. Ye batata hai ki do stationary point charges ke beech mein kitna force lagega. Ye force attractive bhi ho sakta hai aur repulsive bhi.

Statement: Do point charges ke beech lagne wala electrostatic force ($F$), un dono charges ke magnitude ke product ke directly proportional hota hai aur unke beech ki distance ($r$) ke square ke inversely proportional hota hai.

Ye force hamesha un dono charges ke centers ko jodne wali line ke along hi act karta hai. Agar charges ke sign same hain, to wo ek dusre ko dhakelenge (repel), aur agar sign opposite hain, to wo ek dusre ko kheechenge (attract).

TOPIC 2

2. Mathematical Expression of Coulomb's Law

Coulomb's Law ko mathematically niche diye gaye tarike se likha jata hai. Yahan $q_1$ aur $q_2$ do charges hain aur $r$ unke beech ki doori hai:

$$ F = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2} $$

Yahan $k$ ek constant hai jise 'Electrostatic Constant' kehte hain. Vacuum ya air ke liye iski value hoti hai:

$$ k = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \approx 9 \times 10^9 \, Nm^2/C^2 $$

Yahan $\epsilon_0$ (Epsilon Naught) ko "Permittivity of Free Space" kaha jata hai, jiski value $8.854 \times 10^{-12} \, C^2/Nm^2$ hoti hai.

TOPIC 3

3. Force between Multiple Charges (Superposition Principle)

Jab kisi ek jagah par bahut saare charges maujood hon, to kisi ek particular charge par lagne wala total force nikalne ke liye hum **Principle of Superposition** ka sahara lete hain.

Principle: Is principle ke mutabik, kisi ek charge par lagne wala total force, baki sabhi charges dwara us par lagaye gaye individual forces ka **Vector Sum** hota hai.

$$ \vec{F}_{net} = \vec{F}_1 + \vec{F}_2 + \vec{F}_3 + \dots + \vec{F}_n $$

Sabse zaroori baat ye hai ki do charges ke beech ka individual force kisi teesre charge ki maujoodgi (presence) se kabhi bhi change nahi hota.

TOPIC 4

4. Concept of Electric Field

Electric Field kisi bhi charge ke charo taraf ka wo invisible region ya space hota hai jahan tak wo kisi dusre charge par apna asar (force) dikha sakta hai.

Agar koi doosra charge is region mein enter karta hai, to us par hamesha ek electrostatic force lagta hai. Is concept ko Michael Faraday ne diya tha taaki charge-to-charge interaction ko samjha ja sake.

TOPIC 5

5. Electric Field Intensity ($\vec{E}$)

Electric Field Intensity ka matlab hai ki field kisi point par kitna majboot (strong) hai. Ise nikalne ke liye hum ek unit positive test charge ($q_0$) ka use karte hain.

Definition: Kisi point par rakha gaya unit positive test charge jitna force experience karta hai, wahi us point ki electric field intensity hoti hai.

$$ \vec{E} = \frac{\vec{F}}{q_0} $$

Important Points:

  • Ye ek vector quantity hai.
  • Iski direction wahi hoti hai jo positive charge par lagne wale force ki hoti hai.
  • SI Unit: **Newton per Coulomb (N/C)**.

TOPIC 6

6. Electric Field due to Point Charge

Agar hamare paas ek single source charge $Q$ hai, to usse $r$ distance par kisi bhi point par field intensity niche diye gaye formula se nikali jati hai:

$$ E = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{Q}{r^2} $$

Isse ye saaf hota hai ki jaise-jaise distance ($r$) badhega, electric field ki intensity bahut tezi se kam hogi ($E \propto 1/r^2$).

TOPIC 7

7. Electric Field due to Multiple Charges

Multiple charges ke system mein, field nikalne ka tarika waisa hi hai jaisa humne force ke liye kiya tha. Har charge apna alag field produce karta hai, aur net field sabka addition hota hai.

Ise calculate karne ke liye hum vectors ka use karte hain kyunki field ki direction alag-alag ho sakti hai.

$$ \vec{E}_{net} = \vec{E}_1 + \vec{E}_2 + \dots + \vec{E}_n $$
TOPIC 8

8. Superposition Principle of Electric Field

Ye principle final confirmation deta hai ki kisi point par net electric field intensity, wahan maujood sabhi individual fields ka vector sum hoti hai.

Is principle ki wajah se hum bade aur complex systems (jaise dipole ya line charges) ka electric field aasani se calculate kar paate hain. Har charge ka contribution independently add hota hai.

TOPIC 1

1. Electric Field Lines

Electric field lines Michael Faraday dwara diya gaya ek visual concept hai. Ye imaginary curves hoti hain jo hame electric field ki direction aur strength samajhne mein madad karti hain.

Definition: Agar ek unit positive test charge ko field mein free chhod diya jaye, to wo jis raste (path) par chalega, wahi Electric Field Line hai. In lines par kisi bhi point par khinchi gayi **Tangent**, us point par electric field ki direction batati hai.

TOPIC 2

2. Properties of Electric Field Lines

Ye properties exams ke liye bahut important hain:

  • Origin & End: Ye hamesha positive charge se nikalte hain aur negative charge par khatam hote hain.
  • No Intersection: Do lines kabhi ek dusre ko nahi kaat-ti kyunki intersection point par field ki do directions ho jayengi, jo ki impossible hai.
  • Perpendicularity: Ye lines hamesha conductor ki surface se 90 degree par nikalti ya milti hain.
  • No Closed Loops: Ye magnetic lines ki tarah band loop nahi banati, ye sirf positive se negative tak jati hain.
  • Crowding: Jahan lines paas-paas hoti hain wahan field strong hota hai, aur jahan door hoti hain wahan field weak hota hai.
TOPIC 3

3. Field Lines due to Single Charge

Single charge ke liye lines hamesha straight aur radial hoti hain:

  • Positive Charge (+q): Lines radially outward (bahar ki taraf) jati hain up to infinity.
  • Negative Charge (-q): Lines infinity se start hokar radially inward (andar ki taraf) charge par aati hain.
TOPIC 4

4. Field Lines due to Dipole

Ek dipole (+q aur -q ka pair) ke liye field lines positive se start hokar curve banate hue negative charge par end hoti hain. Ye dikhati hain ki dono charges ke beech ek attraction force kaam kar raha hai.

TOPIC 5

5. Electric Dipole

Jab do equal magnitude lekin opposite nature wale charges ($+q$ aur $-q$) ek dusre se bahut choti doori ($2a$) par rakhe hote hain, to is arrangement ko Electric Dipole kehte hain.

Iska total charge zero hota hai, lekin iska electric field zero nahi hota kyunki dono charges ki position alag-alag hoti hai.

TOPIC 6

6. Dipole Moment ($\vec{p}$)

Dipole ki strength ko 'Electric Dipole Moment' se napa jata hai. Ye ek vector quantity hai.

$$ \vec{p} = q \times (2\vec{a}) $$

Unit & Direction:

  • Iski SI Unit **Coulomb-meter (C-m)** hoti hai.
  • Direction: Hamesha **Negative charge se Positive charge** ki taraf hoti hai.

TOPIC 7

7. Electric Field at Axial Position

Dipole ke axis par (dono charges ko jodne wali line par) kisi point $P$ par field intensity jo dipole ke center se $r$ distance par hai:

$$ E_{axial} = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{2pr}{(r^2 - a^2)^2} $$

Short Dipole ($r >> a$) ke liye formula simple ho jata hai:

$$ E_{axial} \approx \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{2p}{r^3} $$
TOPIC 8

8. Electric Field at Equatorial Position

Dipole ke perpendicular bisector (equator) par kisi point par field axial ke mukable half hota hai aur dipole moment ke opposite direction mein hota hai:

$$ E_{equatorial} = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{p}{(r^2 + a^2)^{3/2}} $$

Short Dipole ($r >> a$) ke liye:

$$ E_{equatorial} \approx \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{p}{r^3} $$
TOPIC 9

9. Torque on Dipole in Uniform Field

Jab kisi dipole ko uniform electric field ($E$) mein kisi angle $\theta$ par rakha jata hai, to us par ek turning force lagta hai jise Torque kehte hain.

$$ \tau = \vec{p} \times \vec{E} = pE \sin\theta $$

Special Cases:

  • Maximum Torque: Jab $\theta = 90^\circ$ ($\tau = pE$).
  • Minimum Torque: Jab $\theta = 0^\circ$ ya $180^\circ$ ($\tau = 0$).

TOPIC 10

10. Potential Energy of Dipole ($U$)

Dipole ko field mein rotate karne mein jo work karna padta hai, wo usme Potential Energy ke roop mein store ho jata hai.

$$ U = - \vec{p} \cdot \vec{E} = -pE \cos\theta $$

Stability: Jab $\theta = 0^\circ$ hota hai, to energy minimum hoti hai aur dipole 'Stable Equilibrium' mein hota hai.

TOPIC 1

1. Electric Flux (Concept)

Electric Flux ka matlab hota hai kisi diye gaye area se pass hone wali "Net Electric Field Lines". Ye humein batata hai ki electric field us surface ko kitna "pierce" ya paar kar raha hai.

Ye ek scalar quantity hai, yani iski koi direction nahi hoti, sirf magnitude hota hai. Ise symbol $\Phi$ (Phi) se dikhaya jata hai.

Unit: Iski SI unit $N \cdot m^2/C$ ya $Volt \cdot meter$ ($V \cdot m$) hoti hai.

TOPIC 2

2. Flux through Plane Surface

Agar hamare paas ek flat (plane) surface hai jiska area $A$ hai aur use ek uniform electric field $E$ mein rakha gaya hai, to flux niche diye gaye formula se nikala jata hai:

$$ \Phi = E \cdot A \cos\theta $$

Yahan $\theta$ electric field aur surface ke Area Vector ke beech ka angle hai.

  • Agar field surface ke perpendicular hai ($\theta = 0^\circ$), to flux maximum hoga: $\Phi = EA$.
  • Agar field surface ke parallel hai ($\theta = 90^\circ$), to flux zero hoga: $\Phi = 0$.

TOPIC 3

3. Flux through Closed Surface

Jab surface irregular ya band (closed) hoti hai, jaise ki ek cube ya sphere, to hum pure surface par integration ka use karte hain.

Har ek chote area element ($dA$) ke liye flux calculate karke unhe joda jata hai:

$$ \Phi = \oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} $$

Yahan circle wala integration sign dikhata hai ki integration pure "Closed Surface" par kiya gaya hai.

TOPIC 4

4. Gauss's Law Statement

Gauss's Law electrostatics ka ek bahut hi powerful tool hai. Ye kisi band surface se nikalne wale total flux aur uske andar maujood charge ke beech ka rishta batata hai.

Statement: Kisi bhi closed surface (Gausssian Surface) se nikalne wala total electric flux, us surface ke andar band total charge ($q_{in}$) ka $1/\epsilon_0$ guna hota hai.

$$ \Phi_{total} = \oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} = \frac{q_{enclosed}}{\epsilon_0} $$

Yahan $\epsilon_0$ permittivity of free space hai.

TOPIC 5

5. Applications of Gauss's Law (Overview)

Gauss's law ka sabse bada fayda ye hai ki iski madad se hum complex shapes (jaise lambe taar, badi sheets, ya gole) ka electric field bahut aasani se nikal sakte hain, jahan Coulomb's law lagana mushkil hota hai.

Iske liye hum ek imaginary surface maante hain jise Gaussian Surface kehte hain.

TOPIC 6

6. Field due to Infinite Line Charge

Maan lijiye ek bahut lamba seedha taar (wire) hai jis par charge ek-samaan phaila hua hai. Iski linear charge density $\lambda$ (charge per unit length) hai. Isse $r$ distance par electric field ka formula ye hoga:

$$ E = \frac{\lambda}{2\pi\epsilon_0 r} $$

Isse pata chalta hai ki line charge ke liye electric field distance ke inversely proportional ($E \propto 1/r$) hota hai.

TOPIC 7

7. Field due to Infinite Plane Sheet

Agar hamare paas ek bahut badi patli sheet hai jis par charge phaila hua hai aur uski surface charge density $\sigma$ (charge per unit area) hai, to uske paas kisi bhi point par electric field intensity ka formula hoga:

$$ E = \frac{\sigma}{2\epsilon_0} $$

Note: Ye field distance ($r$) par depend nahi karta. Yani sheet ke paas har jagah field uniform rehta hai.

TOPIC 8

8. Field due to Charged Spherical Shell

Ek khokhle gole (spherical shell) ke liye jis par total charge $Q$ hai aur radius $R$ hai, field teen alag jagahon par nikala jata hai:

A. Outside the Shell ($r > R$): Gole ke bahar field bilkul waise hi act karta hai jaise pura charge center par rakha ho.

$$ E = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{Q}{r^2} $$

B. On the Surface ($r = R$):

$$ E = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{Q}{R^2} $$

C. Inside the Shell ($r < R$): Kyunki gole ke andar koi charge band nahi hai, isliye andar electric field hamesha zero hota hai.

$$ E_{inside} = 0 $$

Q9. Explain the properties of Electric Field Lines in detail.
1. Direction: They start from positive charges and terminate at negative charges.
2. No Intersection: Two field lines never cross. If they did, there would be two directions of field at one point, which is impossible.
3. Continuous Curves: They are smooth curves without any sudden breaks.
4. Tangent: A tangent drawn at any point on the line gives the direction of the electric field at that point.
5. Closed Loops: Electric field lines do not form closed loops (unlike magnetic lines).
