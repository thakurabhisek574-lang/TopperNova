SMART STUDY FOR TOPPERS

1. Atomic Nucleus

English: The central, dense core of an atom containing most of its mass.
Hindi: Atom ka wo beech wala hissa jo bohot bhari aur chota hota hai.
  • Discovered by Lord Rutherford in 1911.
  • It contains protons and neutrons (together called nucleons).
  • Nucleus is positively charged due to protons.
  • The volume of nucleus is very small compared to atom.
$$ \text{Mass} \approx \text{Nucleus} $$

2. Nuclear Size

English: The physical dimensions of a nucleus, usually measured in Fermi.
Hindi: Nucleus kitna bada hai, ise Fermi (fm) mein maapa jata hai.
  • Radius depends on the mass number (A).
  • $1 \, \text{Fermi} = 10^{-15} \, \text{m}$.
  • Typical radius is around $1.2$ to $10 \, \text{fm}$.
  • It is $10^5$ times smaller than the atom's radius.
$$ R = R_0 A^{1/3} $$

3. Nuclear Density

English: The ratio of mass of nucleus to its volume, which is constant.
Hindi: Nucleus ka wazan aur uski jagah ka ratio, jo hamesha fix rehta hai.
  • It is independent of the mass number (A).
  • Value is approx $2.3 \times 10^{17} \, \text{kg/m}^3$.
  • This means nuclear matter is extremely compressed.
  • All nuclei have nearly the same density.
$$ \rho \approx 10^{17} \, \text{kg/m}^3 $$

4. Nuclear Composition

English: The specific makeup of a nucleus in terms of protons and neutrons.
Hindi: Nucleus kin cheezon se milkar bana hai (Protons + Neutrons).
  • Nucleons = Total number of protons and neutrons.
  • Mass is concentrated in these nucleons.
  • Electrons do not exist inside the nucleus.
  • The identity of an element depends on its composition.
$$ \text{Composition} = P + N $$

5. Protons

English: Positively charged subatomic particles found in the nucleus.
Hindi: Nucleus ke andar paye jane wale positive charge wale kan.
  • Discovered by Ernest Rutherford (named in 1920).
  • Charge: $+1.6 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{C}$.
  • Mass is about 1836 times that of an electron.
  • The number of protons determines the Atomic Number (Z).
$$ p = +e $$

6. Neutrons

English: Neutral subatomic particles found in the nucleus with no charge.
Hindi: Nucleus ke wo kan jin par koi charge nahi hota, ye neutral hote hain.
  • Discovered by James Chadwick in 1932.
  • Mass is slightly more than that of a proton.
  • Crucial for the stability of heavier nuclei.
  • Neutrons do not affect the chemical properties.
$$ n = \text{Neutral} $$

7. Atomic & Mass Number

English: Z is the number of protons; A is the sum of protons and neutrons.
Hindi: Z matlab protons ki sankhya, aur A matlab total nucleons.
  • Atomic Number (Z) = Number of Protons.
  • Mass Number (A) = Number of Protons + Neutrons.
  • Number of Neutrons (N) = A - Z.
  • Represented as: $^A_Z X$.
$$ A = Z + N $$

8. Isotopes

English: Atoms of the same element having the same atomic number but different mass numbers.
Hindi: Ek hi element ke wo atoms jinka atomic number (Z) same par mass number (A) alag hota hai.
  • They have an identical number of protons.
  • They have a different number of neutrons.
  • Example: $^{1}_{1}H, ^{2}_{1}H, ^{3}_{1}H$ (Isotopes of Hydrogen).
$$ \text{Same } Z, \text{ Diff } A $$

9. Isobars

English: Atoms of different elements having the same mass number but different atomic numbers.
Hindi: Alag elements ke wo atoms jinka mass number (A) ek jaisa par atomic number (Z) alag hota hai.
  • They have the same number of nucleons.
  • Chemical properties are different.
  • Example: $^{40}_{18}Ar$ and $^{40}_{20}Ca$.
$$ \text{Same } A, \text{ Diff } Z $$

10. Isotones

English: Atoms of different elements having the same number of neutrons.
Hindi: Alag elements ke wo atoms jinme neutrons ki sankhya barabar hoti hai.
  • Value of $(A - Z)$ is constant for them.
  • Both A and Z are different.
  • Example: $^{14}_{6}C$ and $^{16}_{8}O$.
$$ N = A - Z $$

11. Nuclear Force

English: The strong attractive force that binds nucleons together within the nucleus.
Hindi: Wo shaktishali force jo nucleus ke andar protons aur neutrons ko bandhe rakhti hai.
  • Strongest force in nature.
  • Short-range force.
  • Charge-independent.
$$ \text{Strongest Force} $$

12. Properties of Nuclear Force

English: Characteristics including short range, saturation, and charge independence.
Hindi: Nuclear force ki khoobiyan jaise ki short range aur charge independence.
  • Saturation: Interacts only with immediate neighbors.
  • Becomes repulsive if distance < 0.5 fm.
  • Depends on the spin of nucleons.
$$ \text{Short Range} $$

13. Mass Defect

English: Difference between the sum of masses of nucleons and actual nuclear mass.
Hindi: Nucleons ke total wazan aur nucleus ke asli wazan ke beech ka antar.
  • $\Delta m = [Z m_p + (A-Z) m_n] - M_{nucleus}$.
  • Lost mass converts into energy.
$$ \Delta m = \sum m - M $$

14. Binding Energy

English: Energy required to separate nucleons completely from each other.
Hindi: Nucleus ko tod kar uske nucleons ko alag karne ke liye zaruri energy.
  • $BE = \Delta m \cdot c^2$.
  • $BE = \Delta m \times 931.5 \, \text{MeV}$.
$$ E = \Delta m c^2 $$

15. Binding Energy per Nucleon

English: The average energy required to remove a single nucleon from the nucleus.
Hindi: Nucleus se ek single nucleon ko bahar nikalne ki average energy.
  • Formula: $BE/A = \text{Total BE} / \text{Mass Number}$.
  • Indicates the stability of the nucleus.
  • Maximum for Iron ($^{56}Fe$).
$$ \bar{B} = \frac{BE}{A} $$

1. Stability of Nucleus

English: The ability of a nucleus to hold itself together without decaying.
Hindi: Nucleus ki wo kabliyat jisse wo bina toote stable rehta hai.
  • Determined by the ratio of neutrons to protons ($N/Z$ ratio).
  • Stable nuclei fall within the "Band of Stability".

2. Binding Energy Curve

English: A graph showing Binding Energy per nucleon vs Mass Number (A).
Hindi: Ek graph jo har nucleon ki Binding Energy aur mass number ke rishte ko dikhata hai.
  • Peak of the curve is at $^{56}Fe$, making it the most stable nucleus.
  • Light nuclei undergo fusion, and heavy nuclei undergo fission for stability.

3. Radioactivity

English: Spontaneous emission of radiation by unstable nuclei.
Hindi: Unstable nuclei se apne aap nikalne wali radiation ki prakriya.
  • Discovered by Henri Becquerel.
  • It is a nuclear phenomenon, unaffected by temperature or pressure.

4. Radioactive Decay

English: Process where an unstable nucleus loses energy by emitting radiation.
Hindi: Wo process jisme ek nucleus radiation nikal kar doosre element mein badal jata hai.
$$ N = N_0 e^{-\lambda t} $$

5. Half-life ($T_{1/2}$)

English: Time taken for half of the radioactive nuclei in a sample to decay.
Hindi: Wo samay jisme kisi sample ke aadhe nuclei toot jate hain.
$$ T_{1/2} = \frac{0.693}{\lambda} $$

6. Mean Life ($\tau$)

English: The average lifetime of all the nuclei in a radioactive sample.
Hindi: Radioactive sample ke saare nuclei ki average umar.
$$ \tau = \frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.44 \, T_{1/2} $$

7. Decay Constant ($\lambda$)

English: Probability of decay per unit time for a nucleus.
Hindi: Har second ek nucleus ke tootne ki sambhavna.
  • Different for every radioactive element.
  • Unit is $s^{-1}$ or $\text{year}^{-1}$.

8. Alpha Decay ($\alpha$)

English: Emission of a Helium nucleus ($^4_2He$) from an unstable atom.
Hindi: Nucleus se Helium ke kan ($\alpha$-particle) ka nikalna.
  • Mass Number (A) decreases by 4.
  • Atomic Number (Z) decreases by 2.

9. Beta Decay ($\beta$)

English: Emission of electrons ($\beta^-$) or positrons ($\beta^+$) from a nucleus.
Hindi: Nucleus se tez raftaar electron ya positron ka nikalna.
  • Mass Number (A) remains same.
  • Atomic Number (Z) changes by $\pm 1$.

10. Gamma Decay ($\gamma$)

English: Emission of high-energy photons to bring an excited nucleus to ground state.
Hindi: Excited nucleus se high-energy photons (light) ka nikalna.
  • No change in A or Z.
  • Most penetrating power among $\alpha$, $\beta$, and $\gamma$.

1. Law of Radioactive Decay

English: The number of nuclei decaying per unit time is proportional to the total number of nuclei present in the sample.
Hindi: Nuclei ke tutne ki raftaar sample mein maujood kul nuclei ki sankhya ke barabar (proportional) hoti hai.
  • It is a spontaneous and random process.
  • Decay rate depends only on the nature of the element.
  • External factors like temperature don't affect it.
  • The decay constant is represented by lambda ($\lambda$).
  • The negative sign shows that nuclei count is decreasing.
$$ \frac{dN}{dt} = -\lambda N $$

2. Exponential Decay

English: The process where the number of undecayed nuclei decreases exponentially over time.
Hindi: Wo prakriya jisme bache hue nuclei ki sankhya samay ke saath tezi se ghat-ti jati hai.
  • Initially, the decay happens very rapidly.
  • As time passes, the rate of decay slows down.
  • Technically, complete decay takes infinite time.
  • $N_0$ is the number of nuclei at time $t=0$.
  • The graph is a curve that never touches the X-axis.
$$ N = N_0 e^{-\lambda t} $$

3. Nuclear Reactions

English: A reaction that alters the structure, energy, or composition of an atomic nucleus.
Hindi: Wo reaction jo atom ke nucleus ki banawat ya uski energy ko badal deti hai.
  • Total atomic number ($Z$) is conserved.
  • Total mass number ($A$) is always conserved.
  • Mass is converted into energy during the process.
  • Reactions can be spontaneous or induced.
  • They release much more energy than chemical reactions.
$$ \text{Reactants} \rightarrow \text{Products} + \text{Energy} $$

4. Fission

English: Splitting of a heavy nucleus into two or more smaller, stable nuclei.
Hindi: Ek bade aur bhari nucleus ka tut kar do ya zyada halke nuclei mein badalna.
  • Uranium-235 is commonly used for fission.
  • It releases a large amount of heat and neutrons.
  • Basis of nuclear reactors and atomic bombs.
  • Requires a 'critical mass' to sustain the reaction.
  • Produces radioactive waste as a byproduct.
$$ _{92}U^{235} + _0n^1 \rightarrow \text{Fission} $$

5. Fusion

English: Process where light nuclei combine to form a heavier, more stable nucleus.
Hindi: Halke nuclei ka milkar ek bhari aur mazboot nucleus banane ki prakriya.
  • Happens naturally in the Sun and stars.
  • Releases more energy per unit mass than fission.
  • Requires extremely high temperature and pressure.
  • Produces very little radioactive waste.
  • Hydrogen isotopes are the main fuel for fusion.
$$ ^2H + ^3H \rightarrow ^4He + n $$

6. Nuclear Energy

English: The energy stored in the nucleus of an atom, released during reactions.
Hindi: Atom ke nucleus mein chhupi hui urja jo reactions ke waqt nikalti hai.
  • Comes from the conversion of mass into energy.
  • Follows Einstein's mass-energy equivalence.
  • Used for generating electricity in power plants.
  • It is a low-carbon source of energy.
  • Calculated using the mass defect ($\Delta m$).
$$ E = \Delta m c^2 $$

7. Energy Released in Fission

English: The specific amount of energy produced when a heavy atom splits.
Hindi: Ek bhari atom ke tutne par nikalne wali urja ki maatra.
  • About 200 MeV energy per Uranium-235 nucleus.
  • Most of it is Kinetic Energy of fragments.
  • Some energy is released as Gamma radiation.
  • Mass of products is less than the original nucleus.
  • This energy is used to boil water into steam.
$$ \approx 200 \, \text{MeV per Nucleus} $$

8. Energy Released in Fusion

English: Massive energy output generated when light nuclei fuse together.
Hindi: Halke nuclei ke milne par nikalne wali bhari urja.
  • Energy per nucleon is much higher than fission.
  • It powers the core of all active stars.
  • Basis of the Hydrogen Bomb's power.
  • Fuel (like Hydrogen) is abundant on Earth.
  • Clean energy source if controlled effectively.
$$ \text{Sun's Energy Source} $$

1. Applications of Nuclear Energy

English: The use of nuclear fission or fusion to generate heat, electricity, or for specialized industrial and medical purposes.
Hindi: Nuclear reactions ka istemal bijli banane, ya medical aur industrial kamo mein urja ke liye karna.
  • Used in Carbon-14 dating to find age of fossils.
  • Radioisotopes are used to treat cancer (Radiotherapy).
  • Nuclear submarines use this energy to run for years without fuel.
  • Used in agriculture to improve crop quality and pest control.
  • Provides a massive and constant supply of base-load electricity.

2. Nuclear Reactor (Basic Idea)

English: A system designed to maintain and control a sustained nuclear chain reaction to produce power.
Hindi: Ek aisi machine jo nuclear chain reaction ko kabu mein rakh kar bijli paida karti hai.
  • Fuel: Usually uses enriched Uranium-235 or Plutonium.
  • Moderator: Heavy water or Graphite slows down neutrons.
  • Control Rods: Boron or Cadmium rods absorb extra neutrons.
  • Coolant: Water or Liquid Sodium carries away the heat.
  • Safety Shield: Thick concrete walls prevent radiation leaks.
$$ \text{Controlled Chain Reaction} $$

Q6. What are Nuclear Forces? List their 5 main properties with explanation.

1. Definition: Wo strong attractive force jo nucleons (P aur N) ko nucleus ke andar bandh kar rakhta hai.
2. Strongest Force: Ye nature ka sabse takatwar force hai (Gravity se 1038 times strong).
3. Short Range: Ye sirf bahut kam doori (approx 2-3 fm) tak kaam karta hai.
4. Charge Independent: Ye P-P, N-N aur P-N ke beech ek jaisa lagta hai. Charge se koi matlab nahi.
5. Saturation Effect: Ek nucleon sirf apne padosi nucleons par hi force lagata hai.
6. Non-Central Force: Ye sirf center-to-center line par depend nahi karta, spin par bhi depend karta hai.

Q7. Explain Nuclear Fission with an example and explain the Chain Reaction.

1. Jab ek bhari nucleus (heavy nucleus) do halke nuclei mein toot-ta hai, toh use Fission kehte hain.
2. Example: 92U235 + 0n1 → 56Ba141 + 36Kr92 + 3 0n1 + Q (Energy).
3. Is process mein bahut saari energy release hoti hai.
4. Chain Reaction: Fission mein nikalne wale 3 neutrons aage aur 3 Uranium atoms ko todte hain.
5. Ye process multiply hota rehta hai (1 → 3 → 9 → 27...).
6. Agar control na kiya jaye toh ye Atom Bomb ban jata hai (Uncontrolled Chain Reaction).
7. Agar control rods use karein toh ye Nuclear Reactor banta hai (Controlled Chain Reaction).

Q8. What is Nuclear Fusion? Why is very high temperature required for it?

1. Jab do halke nuclei (light nuclei) milkar ek bada nucleus banate hain, toh use Fusion kehte hain.
2. Example: 1H2 + 1H2 → 2He4 + 24 MeV energy.
3. Sun aur Stars mein energy ka source yahi Fusion process hai.
4. High Temp Requirement: Nuclei positive charge hote hain, isliye wo ek doosre ko repel karte hain.
5. Is strong repulsion (Coulomb barrier) ko todne ke liye nuclei ko bahut high kinetic energy chahiye.
6. Ye energy sirf 107 K jaise high temperature par hi milti hai.
7. Isliye Fusion ko "Thermonuclear Reaction" bhi kehte hain.

Q9. Discuss the 4 main features of the Binding Energy per Nucleon curve.

1. Intermediate Nuclei Stability: 30 < A < 170 ke liye B.E./nucleon lagbhag constant (8.0 MeV) hoti hai.
2. Maximum Value: Iron (26Fe56) ki B.E./nucleon sabse zyada (8.8 MeV) hoti hai. Ye sabse stable hai.
3. Low A Nuclei: Halke nuclei (A < 30) ki B.E./nucleon kam hoti hai, isliye wo Fusion karte hain stable hone ke liye.
4. High A Nuclei: Bhari nuclei (A > 170) ki B.E. kam hone lagti hai (Uranium ki ~7.6 MeV), isliye wo Fission karte hain.
5. Conclusion: Curve show karta hai ki nuclei hamesha zyada B.E. (jyada stability) ki taraf jana chahte hain.
