1. Electromagnetic Induction

English: The phenomenon of generating electricity by changing the magnetic field linked with a circuit.
Hinglish: Magnetic field ko change karke conductor me electricity paida karne ki process ko EMI kehte hain.
  • Iska discovery Michael Faraday ne 1831 me kiya tha.
  • Ye mechanical energy ko electrical energy me convert karta hai.
  • Iske bina hum generators aur transformers nahi bana sakte.
$$ \Delta\Phi \rightarrow \text{EMF} $$

2. Induced Current

English: The current produced in a closed circuit due to the change in magnetic flux.
Hinglish: Magnetic flux badalne se jo current behta hai use Induced current kehte hain.
  • Ye current tabhi flow hota hai jab circuit band (closed) ho.
  • Iska direction Fleming's Right Hand rule se pata chalta hai.
  • Ye flux change ka oppose karta hai.
$$ I = \frac{e}{R} $$

3. Induced EMF

English: The potential difference developed across a conductor when flux changes.
Hinglish: Flux badalne se jo voltage ya potential difference paida hota hai use Induced EMF kehte hain.
  • EMF open circuit me bhi paida hota hai.
  • Iski SI unit 'Volt' hoti hai.
  • Ye flux badalne ki raftaar (rate) par depend karta hai.
$$ e = - \frac{d\Phi}{dt} $$

4. Magnetic Flux

English: The total number of magnetic field lines passing through a given area.
Hinglish: Kisi area se guzarne wali total magnetic field lines ko Magnetic Flux kehte hain.
  • Magnetic flux ek scalar quantity hai.
  • Iski SI unit 'Weber' (Wb) hoti hai.
  • Formula B, A aur unke beech ke angle par depend karta hai.
$$ \Phi = B \cdot A \cos \theta $$

5. Change in Magnetic Flux

English: The variation in flux over time which is essential for induction.
Hinglish: Induction hone ke liye flux ka lagatar badalna zaroori hai.
  • Flux ko B, A ya Angle badal kar change kiya ja sakta hai.
  • Jitna fast badlega utna zyada EMF paida hoga.
  • Agar flux constant hai toh induction nahi hoga.
$$ \Delta \Phi = \Phi_2 - \Phi_1 $$

6. Faraday's First Law

English: Whenever there is a change in flux linked with a circuit, an EMF is induced.
Hinglish: Jab bhi flux me badlav hoga, circuit me EMF paida hona tay hai.
  • Ye induction ki condition batata hai.
  • Ye EMF tab tak rehta hai jab tak flux badal raha ho.
$$ \text{Cause: } \frac{d\Phi}{dt} \neq 0 $$

7. Faraday's Second Law

English: The magnitude of induced EMF is equal to the rate of change of magnetic flux.
Hinglish: Paida hone wala EMF kitna hoga, ye flux badalne ki speed se pata chalta hai.
  • Ye quantitative measurement deta hai.
  • EMF is directly proportional to flux change rate.
$$ e \propto \frac{d\Phi}{dt} $$

8. Mathematical Form

English: Combining the observation of flux change with the direction provided by Lenz's Law.
Hinglish: Faraday ke niyam ko math me likhne par Lenz ka (-) sign bhi lagta hai.
  • Negative sign batata hai ki EMF flux change ko rokne ki koshish karta hai.
  • Agar coil me 'N' turns hain toh EMF N guna ho jayega.
$$ e = -N \frac{d\Phi}{dt} $$

1. Lenz’s Law

English: The induced current's direction opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced it.
Hinglish: Induced current hamesha us magnetic flux change ka virodh karta hai jisne use banaya.
  • Based on Conservation of Energy.
  • Determines current direction.
  • Explains the (-) sign in Faraday's Law.
$$ \text{Direction} \propto -\Delta\Phi $$

2. Direction of Induced Current

English: Current flow is found using Fleming's Right Hand Rule or Lenz's Law.
Hinglish: Current clockwise hai ya anti-clockwise, ye Fleming's RHR se pata chalta hai.
  • Thumb: Motion, Forefinger: Field.
  • Middle Finger: Induced Current.
  • Opposes the cause of induction.
$$ \text{Fleming's RHR} $$

3. Motional EMF

English: EMF induced in a conductor moving through a uniform magnetic field.
Hinglish: Jab rod field mein move karti hai, toh voltage banta hai use Motional EMF kehte hain.
  • Converts mechanical energy to electrical.
  • Flux change due to area swept by rod.
  • Depends on velocity and field strength.
$$ e = Bvl $$

4. EMF in Moving Conductor

English: Potential difference across a conductor cutting magnetic field lines.
Hinglish: Jab taar field lines ko cut karta hai, toh uske ends par voltage paida hota hai.
  • Conductor, Field, Velocity are at 90°.
  • No EMF if moving parallel to field.
  • Uses Lorentz force principle.
$$ e = (\vec{v} \times \vec{B}) \cdot \vec{l} $$

5. EMF due to Rotation

English: EMF induced when a conducting rod is rotated in a magnetic field.
Hinglish: Jab kisi rod ko ek kile par gol ghumate hain, toh rotation se EMF banta hai.
  • Uses angular velocity ($\omega$).
  • Observed in cycle spokes.
  • Proportional to length squared ($l^2$).
$$ e = \frac{1}{2} B \omega l^2 $$

6. EMF due to Changing Magnetic Field

English: Induction caused by varying magnetic field strength (B) over time.
Hinglish: Agar conductor ruka rahe par field (B) kam-zyada ho, toh induction hota hai.
  • Called static induction.
  • Rate of change is $dB/dt$.
  • Used in Transformers.
$$ e = -A \frac{dB}{dt} $$

7. EMF due to Changing Area

English: EMF induced by changing the effective area of the coil linked with flux.
Hinglish: Agar hum coil ka area chhota-bada karein, toh EMF generate hota hai.
  • Flux $\Phi = BA$ depends on Area.
  • Seen in sliding wire experiments.
  • Faster area change means more EMF.
$$ e = -B \frac{dA}{dt} $$

8. EMF due to Changing Orientation

English: Induction caused by changing the angle ($\theta$) between field and area.
Hinglish: Jab coil ghumti hai aur angle ($\theta$) badalta hai, toh flux change hota hai.
  • Base of AC Generator.
  • Produces sinusoidal output.
  • Max EMF at $\theta = 90^\circ$.
$$ e = NBA\omega \sin(\omega t) $$

1. Eddy Currents

English: Circulating currents induced in bulk pieces of conductors when subjected to changing magnetic flux.
Hinglish: Jab kisi metal ke bade tukde se juda magnetic flux badalta hai, toh usme gol-gol current paida hota hai jise Eddy Current kehte hain.
  • Also known as Foucault currents.
  • Produced in solid metallic blocks.
  • Discovered by Leon Foucault.
Eddy Currents (Circular loops)

2. Applications of Eddy Currents

English: Practical uses where eddy currents are utilized for braking or heating.
Hinglish: Eddy currents ka istemal breaks lagane aur machines ko garam karne mein kiya jata hai.
  • Magnetic Braking in trains.
  • Induction Furnace (for melting metals).
  • Electromagnetic Damping (Galvanometers).
  • Speedometers.

3. Disadvantages of Eddy Currents

English: Undesired heat loss in electrical devices like transformers and motors.
Hinglish: Eddy currents ki wajah se energy heat ke roop mein barbad hoti hai, jo machines ke liye nuksan-deh hai.
  • Causes excessive heating in cores.
  • Loss of electrical energy.
  • Minimized by using laminated cores.

4. Self Induction

English: The phenomenon where an EMF is induced in a coil due to the change in current through the same coil.
Hinglish: Jab ek hi coil mein current badalne se usi coil mein EMF paida ho jaye, toh ise Self Induction kehte hain.
  • Known as Inertia of Electricity.
  • Opposes the growth or decay of current.
  • Induced EMF is back-EMF.
$$ e = -L \frac{di}{dt} $$

5. Self Inductance (L)

English: A measure of a coil's ability to resist changes in the current flowing through it.
Hinglish: Ye coil ki wo property hai jo current ke badlav ko rokti hai. Iski unit 'Henry' hoti hai.
  • Unit: Henry (H).
  • Depends on geometry and number of turns.
  • Represented by 'L'.
$$ \Phi = Li $$

6. Coefficient of Self Induction

English: The numerical value of self-inductance defined as flux linkage per unit current.
Hinglish: Agar current 1 Ampere ho, toh coil se juda total flux hi Coefficient of Self Induction hota hai.
  • $L = \Phi / i$ when $i = 1A$.
  • $L = e / (di/dt)$ when rate is unit.
  • Purely depends on coil design.
$$ L = \frac{\mu_0 N^2 A}{l} $$

7. Mutual Induction

English: Production of EMF in a secondary coil due to change in current in a nearby primary coil.
Hinglish: Ek coil (primary) mein current badalne par pados wali dusri coil (secondary) mein EMF paida hona.
  • Involves two coils.
  • Principle behind Transformers.
  • Magnetic coupling is essential.
$$ e_s = -M \frac{di_p}{dt} $$

8. Mutual Inductance (M)

English: Property of a pair of coils to oppose any change in current through either of them.
Hinglish: Do coils ke jode ki wo property jo ek dusre ke current change ka virodh karti hai.
  • Unit: Henry (H).
  • Depends on distance between coils.
  • Depends on relative orientation.
$$ \Phi_s = M i_p $$

9. Coefficient of Mutual Induction

English: Defined as the flux linked with one coil when unit current flows in the other.
Hinglish: Ye do coils ke beech ka coupling constant hai. Agar primary mein 1A current ho, toh secondary ka flux 'M' ke barabar hoga.
  • $M = k \sqrt{L_1 L_2}$.
  • Maximum value when coils are wound together.
  • Same for both coils ($M_{12} = M_{21}$).
$$ M = \frac{\mu_0 N_1 N_2 A}{l} $$

1. Inductor

English: A passive electrical component (usually a coil) that stores energy in a magnetic field.
Hinglish: Ye ek coil jaisa component hota hai jo apne andar magnetic field ke roop mein energy store karta hai.
  • Resists sudden changes in current.
  • Symbol: L (Inductance).
  • Ideal inductor has zero resistance.
$$ \text{Symbol: } \text{---}\text{eeee}\text{---} $$

2. Energy Stored in Inductor

English: The potential energy stored in the magnetic field of an inductor when current flows through it.
Hinglish: Jab inductor se current flow hota hai, toh usme kitni magnetic energy jama hoti hai, ye uska formula hai.
  • Stored in the form of magnetic field.
  • Depends on inductance (L) and current squared ($I^2$).
  • Work done against back-EMF is stored as energy.
$$ U = \frac{1}{2} LI^2 $$

3. AC Generator

English: A device that converts mechanical energy into electrical energy in the form of alternating current.
Hinglish: Wo machine jo mechanical energy (ghumne wali energy) ko bijli (AC current) mein badalti hai.
  • Produces Alternating Current (AC).
  • Also known as AC Alternator.
  • Output voltage varies sinusoidally with time.
$$ \text{Output: } e = e_0 \sin(\omega t) $$

4. Working Principle of Generator

English: It works on the principle of Electromagnetic Induction (Faraday's Law).
Hinglish: Ye Electromagnetic Induction par kaam karta hai—jab coil magnetic field mein ghumti hai toh flux change se current banta hai.
  • Based on changing magnetic flux linkage.
  • Relative motion between coil and field.
  • Direction follows Fleming's Right Hand Rule.
$$ \Phi = BA \cos(\theta) $$

5. Construction of Generator

English: Main parts include Armature, Field Magnets, Slip Rings, and Brushes.
Hinglish: Iske main parts hain: Armature (coil), takatwar magnets, Slip Rings aur carbon brushes.
  • Armature: Rectangular coil of many turns.
  • Slip Rings: Rotate with the coil to maintain contact.
  • Brushes: Transfer current to the external circuit.

6. Applications of EMI

English: Everyday technologies that function based on the principles of EMI.
Hinglish: EMI ke principles hamari rozana zindagi ki kayi machines mein use hote hain.
  • Transformers (voltage step-up/down).
  • Induction Cooktops (for cooking).
  • Electric Guitars (pickups).
  • ATM Card Swiping/Scanning.
