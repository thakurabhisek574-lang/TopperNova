1. Electric Current
It is defined as the total amount of electric charge flowing through any cross-section of a conductor per unit time.
I =
Qt

Samjho: Kisi wire mein ek second mein kitna charge flow hota hai, wahi current hai.

2. Direction of Current
The direction of electric current is taken as the direction of flow of positive charges.
Positive → Negative

Samjho: Current hamesha high potential (plus) se low potential (minus) ki taraf jata hai.

3. Conventional Current
It is the current whose direction is opposite to the direction of flow of electrons.
Iconv ∝ - (e- flow)

Samjho: Electrons jis taraf jaate hain, current uske bilkul ulti disha mein mana jata hai.

4. Drift of Electrons
The slow and steady motion of free electrons towards the positive end of a conductor in an electric field.
Electron Movement

Samjho: Field ki wajah se electrons ka thoda-thoda khisakna 'Drift' kehlata hai.

5. Drift Velocity
The average velocity with which free electrons get drifted towards the positive terminal.
vd =
e E τm

Samjho: Wo average speed jis se electrons plus terminal ki taraf bhagte hain.

6. Current Density
It is the amount of current flowing per unit area of cross-section of a conductor. It is a vector.
J =
IA

Samjho: Wire ke har ek chote hisse (area) mein kitna current bhara hua hai.

7. Relation: Current & Density
The total current is the dot product of current density and the area vector.
I = J A cosθ

Samjho: Current density aur area ko multiply karne par humein total Current milta hai.

8. Ohm's Law
Current flowing through a conductor is directly proportional to the potential difference across it.
V = I R

Samjho: Jitna zyada dhakka (V) doge, utna tezz electrons (I) daudenge.

9. V-I Characteristics
The graph between potential difference (V) and current (I). For conductors, it is a straight line.
Slope = R

Samjho: Ye graph batata hai ki Voltage badhane par Current kaise badh raha hai.

10. Ohmic Conductors
Those conductors which strictly obey Ohm's law (e.g., metals like Silver, Copper).
V ∝ I (Linear)

Samjho: Wo cheezein jo Ohm ke niyam ko poori tarah manti hain.

11. Non-Ohmic Conductors
Those conductors which do not obey Ohm's law (e.g., Diode, Transistor, Electrolytes).
V ≠ I R (Non-Linear)

Samjho: Wo cheezein jo Ohm ki baat nahi sunti, inka graph tedha-medha hota hai.

Resistance Section
1. Resistance
The opposition offered by a conductor to the flow of electric current through it.
R =
VI

Samjho: Current ke raste mein aane wali rukawat ko hi resistance kehte hain.

2. Factors Affecting Resistance
Resistance depends on length (L), area (A), nature of material, and temperature.

Samjho: Wire kitni lambi hai, kitni moti hai aur kis cheez ki bani hai, in sabse resistance badalta hai.

3. Resistivity (ρ)
It is the resistance of a conductor of unit length and unit area of cross-section.
ρ =
R AL

Samjho: Ye material ki asli property hai, jo sirf material ke type aur temperature par depend karti hai.

4. Conductivity (σ)
The reciprocal of resistivity is called conductivity. It shows how easily current flows.
σ =
1ρ

Samjho: Ye resistivity ka dushman (ulta) hai. Jitni zyada conductivity, utna makkhan current flow hoga.

5. Relation: R = ρL/A
Formula connecting Resistance with length, area, and resistivity of the material.
R = ρ
LA

Samjho: Wire lambi toh Resistance zyada, wire moti toh Resistance kam.

Temperature Dependence
6. Temp. Dependence of Resistance
For metals, resistance increases with temperature. For semiconductors, it decreases.

Samjho: Garmi badhne par atoms zyada vibrate karte hain, isliye electrons ko nikalne mein dikkat hoti hai.

7. Temp. Coefficient of Resistance (α)
Fractional change in resistance per unit change in temperature.
Rt = Ro(1 + αΔt)

Samjho: Ye α batata hai ki temperature badhne se resistance kitni speed se badhega.

8. Electrical Energy
The total work done by the source in maintaining the current in the circuit.
W = V I t = I2 R t

Samjho: Battery ne poore time mein kitni energy kharch ki load chalane mein.

9. Electric Power
The rate at which electrical energy is dissipated or consumed in an electric circuit.
P =
Wt

10. Power Formula (P = VI)
Power is the product of potential difference and the current.
P = V × I

Samjho: Voltage aur Current ka mel hi Power hai.

11. Power in terms of R and V
Power dissipated across a resistor of resistance R at voltage V.
P =
V2R

Samjho: Agar humein resistance aur voltage pata ho, toh hum isse power nikal sakte hain.

EMF and Potential Difference
1. Electromotive Force (EMF)
EMF is the maximum potential difference between the electrodes of a cell when no current is drawn from it.
E =
Wq

Samjho: Ye battery ki asli taqat hai jab tak wo kisi circuit se judi na ho.

2. Potential Difference (V)
The difference of electrical potential between two points in a circuit when current is flowing.
V = I × R

Samjho: Jab battery kaam kar rahi hoti hai, tab jo voltage milta hai use PD kehte hain.

3. EMF vs Potential Difference
EMF is independent of circuit resistance, while PD depends on the resistance between two points.

Samjho: EMF hamesha PD se bada hota hai (jab battery discharge ho rahi ho).

Internal Resistance
4. Internal Resistance of Cell (r)
The opposition offered by the electrolyte of a cell to the flow of current inside it.
r =
E - VI

Samjho: Battery ke andar ka chemical jo current ko rokta hai, wahi internal resistance hai.

5. Factors Affecting r
It depends on distance between electrodes, area of electrodes, and concentration of electrolyte.

Samjho: Plate kitni door hain aur chemical kitna gaadha hai, isse 'r' badalta hai.

6. Terminal Voltage
The potential difference across the terminals of a cell when current is being drawn from it.
V = E - I r

Samjho: Battery se nikalne ke baad jo final voltage humein bahar milta hai.

Cells and Batteries
7. Primary Cells
Cells in which chemical reactions are irreversible. They cannot be recharged.

Samjho: Use and throw wale cells, jaise remote ka cell.

8. Secondary Cells
Cells in which chemical reactions are reversible. They can be recharged and used again.

Samjho: Rechargeable cells, jaise mobile ki battery ya inverter ki battery.

9. Cells in Series
Cells are connected end-to-end to increase the total voltage of the combination.
Etotal = n E

Samjho: Jab zyada voltage chahiye ho, toh cells ko ek ke piche ek lagate hain.

Cells are connected side-by-side to increase the current capacity.

Samjho: Jab zyada der tak current chahiye ho, toh cells ko parallel mein lagate hain.

Kirchhoff's Laws
1. Kirchhoff’s First Law (Junction Rule)
The algebraic sum of currents meeting at any junction in an electric circuit is zero. It is based on the law of conservation of charge.
∑ I = 0

Samjho: Kisi point (junction) par jitna current aayega, utna hi wahan se bahar jayega.

2. Kirchhoff’s Second Law (Loop Rule)
The algebraic sum of changes in potential around any closed loop is zero. It is based on the law of conservation of energy.
∑ ΔV = 0

Samjho: Ek band raste (loop) mein saare voltages ka total sum hamesha zero hota hai.

6. Meter Bridge
It is the practical form of Wheatstone Bridge used to find the resistance of a wire. It has a 1-meter long uniform wire.

7. Principle of Meter Bridge
It works on the principle of balanced Wheatstone Bridge.

Samjho: Ye bilkul Wheatstone Bridge ke rule par hi kaam karta hai.

Q9. Why is a Potentiometer preferred over a Voltmeter?
REASON 1: NULL METHOD Potentiometer works on null deflection, so it draws no current from the cell. REASON 2: ACCURACY Since no current is drawn, it measures True EMF (E), while a voltmeter measures terminal voltage (V). REASON 3: SENSITIVITY Potentiometer can measure even very small potential differences accurately.
