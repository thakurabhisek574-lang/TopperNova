1. Electromagnetic Waves

English:

Electromagnetic waves are waves that are created as a result of vibrations between an electric field and a magnetic field. They are composed of oscillating magnetic and electric fields that propagate through space.

Hinglish:

EM waves tab banti hain jab electric aur magnetic fields ek doosre ke perpendicular vibrate karti hain. Inhe travel karne ke liye kisi medium ki zarurat nahi hoti.

  • Non-mechanical in nature (can travel in vacuum).
  • Transverse waves where fields are at 90°.
  • Travel at the speed of light ($3 \times 10^8$ m/s).
$$ c = \lambda \nu $$

2. Displacement Current

English:

It is a type of current that arises in a region where the electric field and electric flux are changing with time. It exists in addition to conduction current to maintain continuity in circuits like capacitors.

Hinglish:

Ye current tab aata hai jab electric flux badalta hai. Ye capacitor ki plates ke beech mein paida hota hai jahan conduction current zero hota hai.

  • Proposed by Maxwell to fix Ampere's law.
  • Depends on the rate of change of electric flux.
  • Produces a magnetic field just like conduction current.
$$ I_d = \epsilon_0 \frac{d\Phi_E}{dt} $$

3. Need for Displacement Current

English:

The need for displacement current arises because Ampere's circuital law was inconsistent for non-steady currents. It was required to explain how magnetic fields exist between capacitor plates where no charges flow.

Hinglish:

Ampere's law ko poora karne ke liye iski zarurat padi. Iske bina hum ye nahi samjha sakte the ki capacitor ki plates ke beech magnetic field kaise banta hai.

  • Makes Ampere's law logically consistent.
  • Explains the magnetic field in gap regions.
  • Completes the Maxwell-Ampere equation.
$$ \oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0 (I_c + I_d) $$

4. Maxwell's Equations (Basic Idea)

English:

Maxwell's equations are a set of four fundamental laws that unify electricity and magnetism. They describe how electric charges produce electric fields and how currents produce magnetic fields.

Hinglish:

Maxwell ki 4 equations poori electromagnetism ka aadhar hain. Inse pata chalta hai ki electric aur magnetic fields kaise ek doosre ko badalti hain.

  • Unifies all laws of electricity and magnetism.
  • Predicts the existence of EM waves.
  • Shows light itself is an electromagnetic wave.
$$ \text{Maxwell's 4 Equations} $$

5. Electromagnetic Wave Concept

English:

The concept states that an oscillating charge produces an oscillating electric field, which in turn produces an oscillating magnetic field. This cycle creates a wave that propagates through space.

Hinglish:

Concept ye hai ki accelerated charge ek electric field badalta hai, jisse magnetic field banta hai, aur isi tarah wave aage badhti rehti hai.

  • Oscillating charges are the source.
  • Fields sustain each other without a medium.
  • Energy is transferred through these oscillations.
$$ \text{Source: Accelerated Charge} $$

6. Nature of EM Waves

English:

EM waves are non-mechanical waves that do not require any material medium for propagation. They carry energy and momentum and exert pressure (radiation pressure) on surfaces they strike.

Hinglish:

EM waves ki nature aisi hai ki ye vacuum mein bhi travel kar sakti hain. Inme energy aur momentum dono hota hai aur ye light ki tarah behave karti hain.

  • Electrically neutral (no deflection in fields).
  • Carry energy from source to receiver.
  • Follow the principle of superposition.
$$ \text{Energy Density } u = \epsilon_0 E^2 $$

7. Transverse Nature

English:

The transverse nature means that the oscillations of the electric and magnetic fields are perpendicular to each other and also perpendicular to the direction in which the wave is moving.

Hinglish:

Transverse nature ka matlab hai ki fields wave ke chalne ki disha ke 90 degree par vibrate karti hain. Ye polarization se prove hota hai.

  • E and B are always in the same phase.
  • Direction of wave is given by $\vec{E} \times \vec{B}$.
  • Distinguishes EM waves from sound waves.
$$ \vec{E} \cdot \vec{k} = 0, \vec{B} \cdot \vec{k} = 0 $$

8. Speed of EM Waves

English:

The speed of EM waves in vacuum is a fundamental constant. It is determined by the permeability ($\mu_0$) and permittivity ($\epsilon_0$) of free space, resulting in approximately $3 \times 10^8$ m/s.

Hinglish:

EM waves ki speed vacuum mein light ki speed ke barabar hoti hai. Medium badalne par iski speed $\mu$ aur $\epsilon$ ki wajah se kam ho jati hai.

  • In vacuum: $c = 1/\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}$.
  • Independent of frequency in vacuum.
  • Determines the refractive index of materials.
$$ v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu \epsilon}} $$

9. Relation between E and B

English:

In an EM wave, the magnitudes of the electric field (E) and magnetic field (B) are related to each other by the speed of light. They reach their maximum and minimum values at the same time.

Hinglish:

Electric field aur Magnetic field ka ratio hamesha light ki speed (c) ke barabar hota hai. E ki value B se bahut zyada badi hoti hai magnitude mein.

  • Ratio $E_0 / B_0 = c$.
  • Both fields are in the same phase.
  • Energy is equally shared between E and B.
$$ E = cB $$

10. Energy of EM Waves

English:

EM waves carry energy as they travel through space. This energy is shared equally between the electric and magnetic field components and can be absorbed by objects in their path.

Hinglish:

EM waves apne saath energy le kar chalti hain. Ye energy half-half divide hoti hai electric aur magnetic fields ke beech mein.

  • Total energy density $u = u_E + u_B$.
  • Energy is proportional to the square of amplitude.
  • Intensity is the energy per unit area per second.
$$ u_{avg} = \frac{1}{2} \epsilon_0 E_0^2 $$

1. Electromagnetic Spectrum

English:

The electromagnetic spectrum is the range of all types of EM radiation. Each type of radiation in the spectrum has a unique wavelength and frequency, ranging from long radio waves to very short gamma rays.

Hinglish:

Electromagnetic spectrum sari EM waves ki ek range hai. Isme waves ko unki frequency aur wavelength ke hisaab se lagaya jata hai, jaise Radio waves se lekar Gamma rays tak.

  • Includes visible and invisible light.
  • Classified by frequency and wavelength.
  • All travel at speed of light in vacuum.
$$ \text{Range: } 10^4 \text{ to } 10^{22} \text{ Hz} $$

2. Order of EM Waves

English:

The standard order of EM waves (increasing frequency) is: Radio waves, Microwaves, Infrared, Visible light, Ultraviolet, X-rays, and Gamma rays. This order helps determine the energy of each wave.

Hinglish:

EM waves ka sahi order hai: Radio, Micro, Infrared, Visible, UV, X-rays aur Gamma rays. Jaise-jaise frequency badhti hai, wave ki energy bhi badhti jati hai.

  • Radio waves have lowest frequency.
  • Gamma rays have highest frequency.
  • Wavelength follows the opposite order.
$$ \text{Radio } \rightarrow \text{ Visible } \rightarrow \text{ Gamma} $$

3. Radio Waves

English:

Radio waves are produced by accelerated motion of charges in wires. They have the longest wavelength and are primarily used for radio and television communication systems.

Hinglish:

Radio waves antennas mein charges ke vibrate hone se banti hain. Inka wavelength sabse lamba hota hai aur ye TV aur Radio signals bhejne ke kaam aati hain.

  • Wavelength: > 0.1 m.
  • Used in AM/FM radio broadcasting.
  • Used in cellular phone communication.
$$ \lambda > 1 \text{ meter} $$

4. Microwaves

English:

Microwaves are short-wavelength radio waves produced by special vacuum tubes. They are ideal for radar systems and for heating food in microwave ovens by vibrating water molecules.

Hinglish:

Microwaves choti radio waves hoti hain. Inka use Radar technology mein jahazon ka rasta dekhne aur kitchen mein khana garam karne ke liye kiya jata hai.

  • Frequency: $10^9$ to $10^{12}$ Hz.
  • Used in satellite communication.
  • Used in Radar for aircraft navigation.
$$ \text{Radar & Oven Use} $$

5. Infrared Waves

English:

Infrared waves are produced by hot bodies and molecules. Often called 'heat waves', they are used in remote controls, night vision goggles, and keeping the Earth warm via the greenhouse effect.

Hinglish:

Infrared waves garam cheezon se nikalti hain, isiliye inhe "heat waves" kehte hain. TV remote aur night vision cameras mein yahi waves use hoti hain.

  • Wavelength: 700 nm to 1 mm.
  • Used in physical therapy.
  • Responsible for Greenhouse Effect.
$$ \text{Heat Radiation} $$

6. Visible Light

English:

Visible light is the part of the spectrum that our eyes can detect. It ranges from violet to red. It allows us to see the world around us and is essential for photosynthesis.

Hinglish:

Visible light spectrum ka wo hissa hai jise hum dekh sakte hain. Isme VIBGYOR ke saare rang hote hain aur ye humein cheezein dekhne mein madad karti hai.

  • Range: 400 nm to 700 nm.
  • Smallest part of the spectrum.
  • Source: Sun, light bulbs, etc.
$$ \text{Visible Range: VIBGYOR} $$

7. Ultraviolet Rays

English:

Ultraviolet (UV) rays are high-energy waves from the sun. While they help in water purification and sterilization, overexposure can cause skin damage and eye problems. The ozone layer absorbs most UV rays.

Hinglish:

UV rays suraj se aati hain aur inme kafi energy hoti hai. Inka use bacteria maarne aur water purification (RO) mein hota hai, lekin ye skin ke liye nuksan-deh ho sakti hain.

  • Wavelength: 1 nm to 400 nm.
  • Used in LASIK eye surgery.
  • Absorbed by the Earth's Ozone layer.
$$ \text{Source: Sun & UV Lamps} $$

8. X-rays

English:

X-rays are highly penetrating radiation used in medical imaging to view bones. They are produced by bombarding a metal target with high-energy electrons.

Hinglish:

X-rays bahut takatwar hoti hain aur sharir ke andar dekhne ke kaam aati hain. Inka main use hospital mein tuti hui haddiyon ka pata lagane ke liye hota hai.

  • High penetrating power.
  • Used in airport security scanners.
  • Used in CT Scans for diagnosis.
$$ \text{Medical Imaging Use} $$

9. Gamma Rays

English:

Gamma rays have the shortest wavelength and highest frequency. They are produced in nuclear reactions and by radioactive nuclei. They are used in radiotherapy to destroy cancer cells.

Hinglish:

Gamma rays sabse zyada energy wali waves hain jo nuclear reactions se nikalti hain. Inka use cancer ke ilaaj (radiotherapy) mein cells ko khatam karne ke liye hota hai.

  • Highest energy in the spectrum.
  • Produced by radioactive decay.
  • Used for sterilizing medical equipment.
$$ E = h \nu \text{ (Maximum)} $$

1. Uses of Radio Waves

English:

Radio waves are extensively used for long-distance communication. They are the backbone of radio broadcasting (AM/FM), television signal transmission, and cellular phone networks because they can travel through the atmosphere easily.

Hinglish:

Radio waves ka sabse bada use communication mein hota hai. TV ke signals, FM radio, aur hamare mobile phone ki calling isi wave ki wajah se possible hai kyunki ye lambi doori tak asani se ja sakti hain.

  • Used in Wireless Communication.
  • Standard for TV and Radio signals.
  • Vital for Air Traffic Control.
$$ \text{Communication Backbone} $$

2. Uses of Microwaves

English:

Microwaves are used in radar systems for aircraft navigation and speed detection by police. In households, they are used in microwave ovens to cook food quickly by vibrating water molecules inside the food.

Hinglish:

Microwaves ka use Radar systems mein jahazon ki direction pata karne ke liye hota hai. Saath hi, ye Microwave Ovens mein khana banane aur satellite signals bhejane ke liye bhi best hain.

  • Used in Aircraft Radar.
  • Used in Microwave Ovens.
  • Effective for Point-to-Point Communication.
$$ \text{Radar & Satellite Link} $$

3. Uses of Infrared

English:

Infrared waves are used in short-range communication like TV remote controls. They are also used in thermal imaging cameras to see in the dark and in physical therapy to treat muscular strains by providing heat.

Hinglish:

Infrared ka use hamare TV remote mein hota hai. Inhe "Heat waves" bhi kehte hain, isiliye night vision cameras aur doctor body pain thik karne ke liye inka use karte hain.

  • Used in TV Remote Controls.
  • Night Vision Technology.
  • Horticulture (Greenhouses).
$$ \text{Heat / Thermal Sensing} $$

4. Uses of Visible Light

English:

Visible light is essential for human vision, allowing us to perceive the world. It is also used in photography, fiber optic communication to transmit high-speed data, and is critical for photosynthesis in plants.

Hinglish:

Visible light ki wajah se hi hum duniya dekh paate hain. Iska use photography aur high-speed internet (Optical Fiber) mein hota hai. Ped-poudhe isi se apna khana banate hain.

  • Enables Vision.
  • Optical Fiber Communication.
  • Used in Photography & Lasers.
$$ \text{Vision & Fiber Optics} $$

5. Uses of Ultraviolet

English:

Ultraviolet (UV) radiation is used for water purification as it kills bacteria and germs. It is also used in LASIK eye surgery, to detect counterfeit currency notes, and to sterilize surgical instruments in hospitals.

Hinglish:

UV rays ka use bacteria marne ke liye hota hai (RO filters mein). Hospitals mein auzaron ko saaf karne aur nakli notes ki pehchan karne ke liye bhi UV light ka istemal hota hai.

  • Water Sterilization (RO).
  • Detecting Fake Currency.
  • Used in Forensic Investigation.
$$ \text{Sterilization & Security} $$

6. Uses of X-rays

English:

X-rays are primarily used in the medical field to diagnose bone fractures and infections. In security, they are used at airports to scan luggage, and in industries to detect cracks in metal structures.

Hinglish:

X-rays ka sabse bada use hospital mein haddi (bone) tutne par photo lene ke liye hota hai. Airport par luggage checking aur badi machines ke andar ki dararen check karne mein bhi ye kaam aati hain.

  • Medical Radiography (Fractures).
  • Airport Security Scanning.
  • Cancer treatment (Radiotherapy).
$$ \text{Diagnostic Imaging} $$

7. Uses of Gamma Rays

English:

Gamma rays are used in medicine to destroy cancer cells in radiotherapy. They are also used in the food industry to kill pests and bacteria in packed food to increase its shelf life without affecting its taste.

Hinglish:

Gamma rays cancer ke ilaaj mein bahut zaruri hain. Ye packed food ko bacteria-free banane aur nuclear research mein use hoti hain kyunki ye bahut takatwar hoti hain.

  • Cancer Radiotherapy.
  • Food Preservation.
  • Nuclear Research & Medicine.
$$ \text{High Energy Therapy} $$

8. Wavelength

English:

Wavelength ($\lambda$) is the distance between two consecutive peaks (crests) or troughs in a wave. In the EM spectrum, radio waves have the longest wavelength while gamma rays have the shortest.

Hinglish:

Wavelength do crests ya do troughs ke beech ki doori ko kehte hain. Isse hum wave ki "lambai" samajh sakte hain. Radio waves ki wavelength sabse badi hoti hai.

  • Represented by $\lambda$.
  • S.I. Unit is Meter (m).
  • Inverse to Frequency.
$$ \lambda = \frac{v}{f} $$

9. Frequency

English:

Frequency ($f$ or $\nu$) is the number of wave oscillations that pass a fixed point in one second. Higher frequency means the wave carries more energy, which is why Gamma rays are so dangerous.

Hinglish:

Ek second mein kitni waves guzar rahi hain, usey frequency kehte hain. Frequency jitni zyada hogi, wave ki energy bhi utni hi zyada hogi.

  • Represented by $f$ or $\nu$.
  • S.I. Unit is Hertz (Hz).
  • Determines Wave Energy.
$$ f = \frac{1}{T} $$

10. Wave Speed Relation

English:

In vacuum, all EM waves travel at the speed of light ($c$). The speed of a wave is always equal to the product of its frequency and wavelength. If wavelength increases, frequency must decrease to keep the speed constant.

Hinglish:

Vacuum mein har EM wave light ki speed se chalti hai. Speed hamesha frequency aur wavelength ke guna (product) ke barabar hoti hai. Agar wavelength badhegi, toh frequency kam ho jayegi.

  • $c \approx 3 \times 10^8$ m/s.
  • Speed remains constant in vacuum.
  • Fundamental wave equation.
$$ c = \nu \lambda $$

Q8. Briefly explain Hertz's experiment and how it proved Maxwell’s theory.

1. Maxwell predicted EM waves theoretically, but Hertz proved them experimentally.
2. Hertz used an oscillatory LC circuit to produce sparks between two metal spheres.
3. These sparks created EM waves that were detected by a receiver loop at a distance.
4. He showed these waves could be reflected and refracted like light.
5. He calculated the speed of these waves using v = f λ.
6. The speed was found to be 3 × 108 m/s, matching the speed of light.
7. This confirmed that light is indeed an Electromagnetic Wave.
